Comparison of ABC, Genetic Algorithm, and Hybrid for TSP

Artificial Bee Colony (ABC) Algorithm

The ABC algorithm is inspired by the foraging behavior of bees. It excels in exploitation, focusing on refining solutions within a local search space. For the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP), ABC efficiently fine-tunes routes by iteratively improving paths through employed and onlooker bee phases. However, its exploration capability is limited, which may lead to getting stuck in local optima.

Genetic Algorithm (GA)

The Genetic Algorithm mimics natural evolution through selection, crossover, and mutation. It is highly effective for exploration, as it maintains a diverse population of solutions and explores the global search space. In TSP, GA generates varied routes but may converge slowly due to less emphasis on local optimization.

Hybrid ABC-GA Approach

The hybrid approach combines the strengths of ABC and GA to achieve an optimal balance between exploration and exploitation. GA's diverse population explores the solution space, while ABC's local search refines promising routes. This synergy often leads to optimal solutions for TSP, with faster convergence and better route quality compared to standalone ABC or GA.
